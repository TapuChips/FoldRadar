The best foldable phones in 2026

1. Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 — best for most people

Seven generations in, the Z Fold 7 is the foldable with the fewest asterisks: 8.9mm closed, 215g, a 200MP main camera that finally matches Samsung's slab flagships, and the most mature big-screen software on Android. App support is a solved problem here. It launched at $1,999 and discounts are common — especially as new rivals land.

2. Google Pixel 10 Pro Fold — most durable

The first foldable with a real IP68 rating and a gearless hinge, plus world-first Qi2 magnetic wireless charging in a foldable. If your last foldable died from dust or a beach trip, this is the one that shrugs it off. Pixel camera processing and seven years of updates round it out.

3. Honor Magic V5 — thinnest, best cameras

At 4.1mm unfolded (Ivory White), the Magic V5 is the world's thinnest book-style foldable, with a full Snapdragon 8 Elite, 16GB RAM, and DXOMARK's top-ranked foldable camera. Reviewers at Tom's Guide put it level with the Z Fold 7 and Pixel 10 Pro Fold. The catch for US readers: availability is import-only; in the UK and Germany it sells around £1,699/€1,999.

4. The flip options: Galaxy Z Flip 7 and Motorola Razr Ultra

If "foldable" for you means pocketable rather than tablet-sized, the Z Flip 7's larger cover display and the Razr Ultra's flagship internals are the two to compare. Both are hundreds cheaper than book-style foldables and both are fashion-forward daily drivers rather than productivity machines.

Should you just wait for the iPhone Fold?

If you're in the Apple ecosystem: yes — it's expected in September 2026 at around $1,999, and buying a $2,000 Android foldable weeks before Apple's entry is bad timing both ways (you'll want the Apple one, and the Android one will get discounted). If you're committed to Android, buy now with confidence — today's foldables are genuinely finished products, and Apple's launch will push their prices down, not up.
