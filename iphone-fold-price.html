How much will the iPhone Fold cost?

Last updated: July 20, 2026

Answer: about $1,999 to start, according to the most recent reports — notably down from the $2,399 floated through 2025. Storage is expected to run 256GB to 1TB. Estimated regional pricing: ~£1,899 (UK), ~€2,299–2,399 (Germany). Nothing is official.

The UK and Germany figures are our estimates from Apple's consistent regional pricing patterns (an iPhone 17 Pro Max at $1,199 lists at €1,449, for example). Apple sets European prices at announcement; we'll replace estimates with real numbers the day they exist.

Why the price came down

Early 2025 reporting clustered around $2,399–2,499. Through late 2025 and 2026, analyst notes revised toward $1,999 — driven, reportedly, by Apple wanting to match the Galaxy Z Fold 7's launch price rather than sit above it, and by better-than-expected display yields. A $1,999 iPhone Fold would be Apple's most expensive iPhone ever, but only by $200 over the top iPhone 17 Pro Max configuration.

What you'd get for it

For context, $1,999 buys the rumored 256GB base model: ~7.8-inch crease-free inner display, A20 Pro on 2nm with 12GB RAM, dual 48MP cameras, titanium body — the full breakdown is in the iPhone Fold tracker. Reports describe storage tiers up to 1TB, which would likely push the top configuration past $2,500.
